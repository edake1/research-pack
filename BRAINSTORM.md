# ResearchPack - Brainstorming Session

## The Origin Story

**Original Insight:** In college, every research project starts from scratch. You search Google, YouTube, academic databases, read books, watch videos, discard the bad ones, keep the good ones... and someone else has already done this exact same work. Why can't we inherit that effort instead of repeating it?

**The Vision:** A platform that sits at the intersection of GitHub and YouTube — where research processes are shared, forked, improved, and credited.

---

## The Core Problem

### What's Broken?

**The Duplication of Research Effort:**

Every person researching a topic goes through the SAME stages:
1. 🔍 **Search** — Find potential sources
2. 🧹 **Filter** — Discard the irrelevant/low-quality ones
3. 📖 **Consume** — Read/watch/listen
4. 🧠 **Synthesize** — Connect the dots
5. 📝 **Conclude** — Form takeaways

This happens **millions of times** for the **same topics**. That's massive duplicated effort.

**Example:**
- 10,000 students research "quantum computing basics" this year
- Each spends ~5 hours on search and filtering
- That's 50,000 hours of duplicated work
- What if the first person packaged their research and the other 9,999 could build on it?

### Why Now?

1. **Information Overload is Worse Than Ever**
   - More content exists than ever before
   - Finding quality amidst noise is the real challenge
   - Curation is more valuable than raw search

2. **AI is Changing Search Behavior**
   - People use ChatGPT/Perplexity instead of Google
   - But AI hallucinates and doesn't show the research trail
   - Opportunity: Human curation + AI assistance, not replacement

3. **Remote Work/Study is Normalized**
   - People are more isolated in their learning
   - Need connection over shared intellectual interests
   - Asynchronous collaboration is expected

4. **Creator Economy Maturation**
   - People want credit for intellectual labor, not just creative output
   - Attribution and recognition matter more than ever

## Why Not Some Other Platform?

### vs. Google / Perplexity / ChatGPT

| Aspect | Google/Perplexity/ChatGPT | ResearchPack |
|--------|---------------------------|--------------|
| Output | A list of links OR an AI answer | A curated, contextualized research bundle |
| Curation | None (algorithmic ranking only) | Human judgment and context |
| Attribution | Sources listed, but no credit for finding them | Researcher gets credit for the curation work |
| Iteration | Start fresh every time | Fork and improve existing research |
| Transparency | AI black box | See every source and why it was included |
| Community | None | Researchers helping researchers |

**Key Differentiator:** We value the *process* of research, not just the output.

### vs. Notion / Roam / Obsidian

| Aspect | Note-Taking Apps | ResearchPack |
|--------|------------------|--------------|
| Visibility | Private by default | Public, discoverable |
| Purpose | Personal knowledge management | Shared knowledge building |
| Attribution | N/A | Built-in credit system |
| Discovery | None | Search and browse |
| Community | None | Social layer |

**Key Differentiator:** Your notes become a public good that others can build on.

### vs. Wikipedia

| Aspect | Wikipedia | ResearchPack |
|--------|-----------|--------------|
| Perspective | Consensus view, neutral | Individual perspectives welcome |
| Attribution | Collective, no individual credit | Individual researchers credited |
| Sources | Listed at bottom | Central to the experience with context |
| Number of Views | One article per topic | Multiple "takes" on the same topic |
| Formality | Formal, cited | Can be casual, blog-style |
| Forking | Edit wars | Clean forks with attribution |

**Key Differentiator:** Personal research journeys, not consensus documents.

### vs. Academia / ResearchGate

| Aspect | Academic Platforms | ResearchPack |
|--------|-------------------|--------------|
| Access | Often paywalled, credentialed | Open, accessible to all |
| Sources | Academic papers only | Anything: YouTube, blogs, podcasts, papers |
| Speed | Slow, peer-reviewed | Fast, community-validated |
| Gatekeeping | Credentials required | Anyone can contribute |

**Key Differentiator:** Democratized research, not just for academics.

### vs. YouTube

| Aspect | YouTube | ResearchPack |
|--------|---------|--------------|
| Format | Video-first | Research-first (any media) |
| Sources | Hard to see, often in description | Central, structured, contextualized |
| Attribution | Creator gets views | Creator gets research credit |
| Iteration | Comments only | Fork and build upon |
| Depth | Single video perspective | Synthesized from multiple sources |

**Key Differentiator:** Research is the product, not video content.

## The AI Integration (The "Now" Factor)

### The Philosophy: AI as Assistant, Not Replacement

AI should make research pack creation **easier**, not replace human judgment. The value of a pack is the human curation — knowing what to include, what to skip, and how to contextualize.

### AI Features We'll Build

#### 1. AI Research Assistant (Pack Creation)
**When creating a pack:**
- Enter your topic
- AI searches the web and suggests relevant sources
- AI summarizes long papers/videos you add
- AI helps extract key takeaways
- **Human reviews and approves everything**

**Why this matters:** Reduces the friction of creating a pack. The hardest part is starting. AI helps you start, but you make the final call.

#### 2. AI-Powered Search & Discovery
**When searching for packs:**
- Natural language search ("I need to understand quantum computing for a beginner")
- AI finds relevant packs even if keywords don't match exactly
- AI can answer questions based on pack contents

**Why this matters:** Better discovery means more reuse of existing research.

#### 3. AI Summarization (Pack Consumption)
**When viewing a pack:**
- "TL;DR" generated from the full pack
- Quick summaries of each source
- "What changed" summaries when viewing forks

**Why this matters:** Helps users quickly assess if a pack is relevant to them.

### What We WON'T Do With AI

- ❌ Auto-generate packs without human oversight
- ❌ Replace human curation and judgment
- ❌ Hide when AI was used vs. human work
- ❌ Let AI be the sole author of research

### The Transparency Principle

Every pack shows:
- Who created it (human)
- Which parts had AI assistance
- The full research trail (every source, every decision)

**Trust comes from transparency.**

## Feature Scope

### Must Have (MVP - This Weekend)

1. **Create a Research Pack**
   - Title, description, topic
   - Add sources (URLs, papers, videos)
   - Add notes and context for each source
   - Key takeaways section

2. **Search & Discovery**
   - Search by topic, keyword
   - Browse by category/tags
   - See trending/popular packs

3. **View Pack Details**
   - See all sources with notes
   - See key takeaways
   - See pack creator

4. **Fork a Pack**
   - Create your own version of someone's pack
   - Add your own sources, notes
   - Attribution to original creator

5. **Basic Profiles**
   - User profile with their packs
   - See their contributions

6. **Rating/Thanks System**
   - "This helped me" button
   - Simple quality signal

7. **ONE AI Feature**
   - AI-assisted source suggestions during pack creation

### Should Have (Next Iteration)

1. Comments/discussion on packs
2. Pack version history
3. Export packs (PDF, Markdown)
4. Pack collections (group related packs)
5. Advanced AI features (summarization, TL;DR)

### Nice to Have (Future)

1. Expert directory / consultation booking
2. Pack analytics (views, forks, saves)
3. Collaborative packs (multiple contributors)
4. Browser extension to save sources
5. Integration with reference managers (Zotero, Mendeley)
6. Verification badges for subject matter experts
7. Private packs (for internal use)

### Not Doing (At Least Not Now)

1. Video hosting (embed YouTube/Vimeo)
2. Full paper hosting (link to sources)
3. Formal peer review system
4. Monetization/premium tiers
5. Native mobile app

## Open Questions (To Keep Thinking About)

1. **Quality Control:** How do we prevent low-quality packs from flooding the platform?
   - Idea: Reputation system, community moderation
   - MVP: Simple rating system

2. **Incentives:** Why would people share their research?
   - Recognition, attribution, building reputation
   - Future: Expert consultation marketplace

3. **Academic Credibility:** Will academics take this seriously?
   - Start with students and independent researchers
   - Build credibility through quality

4. **Copyright:** What about sharing paywalled papers?
   - Link to sources, don't host content
   - Notes and takeaways are original work

5. **Competition:** What if Perplexity or Notion adds this feature?
   - First-mover advantage in this specific niche
   - Community and network effects matter
   - Different DNA (research-first vs. search-first)
